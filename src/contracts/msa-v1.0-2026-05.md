# Master Services Agreement

**Version:** msa-v1.0-2026-05
**Between:** All Care 4 U Group Ltd, a company incorporated in England & Wales (company number 09428739), trading as **SpecialCarer** ("**SpecialCarer**", "we", "us")
**And:** the customer organisation identified at signature ("**Customer**", "you")

This Master Services Agreement ("**MSA**" or "**Agreement**") sets out the terms on which SpecialCarer makes its platform available to the Customer for the purpose of finding, booking and managing independent care professionals.

---

## 1. Definitions

1.1 **"Booking"** means a confirmed engagement of a Carer by the Customer through the Platform.
1.2 **"Carer"** means an independent care professional onboarded by SpecialCarer who provides services to Customers through the Platform.
1.3 **"DPA"** means the Data Processing Addendum at Schedule 1 to this Agreement.
1.4 **"Fees"** means the amounts payable by the Customer for Bookings, calculated under section 5 and Schedule 2.
1.5 **"Platform"** means the SpecialCarer web and mobile applications, APIs and supporting systems.
1.6 **"Service Users"** means the individuals to whom the Customer provides care and on whose behalf the Customer makes Bookings.
1.7 **"Services"** means the platform services provided by SpecialCarer to the Customer under this Agreement.

## 2. Services

2.1 SpecialCarer will make the Platform available to the Customer to:
  (a) browse vetted Carers;
  (b) create Bookings;
  (c) communicate with Carers;
  (d) manage payments via Stripe Invoicing.

2.2 The Customer is responsible for using the Platform in line with this Agreement and any in-platform guidance.

## 3. Carer status

3.1 Carers are independent professionals. **Neither SpecialCarer nor the Customer is the employer of a Carer** unless separately and explicitly agreed in writing.
3.2 SpecialCarer is responsible for vetting Carers (see section 6); the Customer is responsible for direction and supervision of Carers during a Booking to the extent required by the relevant care regime.

## 4. Term and termination

4.1 The initial term of this Agreement is **twelve (12) months** from the Effective Date.
4.2 The Agreement renews automatically for further twelve-month periods unless either party gives **30 days' written notice** of non-renewal before the end of the then-current term.
4.3 Either party may terminate immediately for material breach not remedied within 30 days of written notice, or for the other party's insolvency.
4.4 SpecialCarer may suspend access for non-payment, suspected fraud, or material safeguarding concerns; we will give reasons in writing where lawful to do so.

## 5. Fees and invoicing

5.1 Customer-facing rates per Carer are listed on the Carer's profile and on Schedule 2.
5.2 The platform fee is **25% of the Carer's hourly rate, deducted from the Carer's payout**. There is **no surcharge added to the Customer's invoice**.
5.3 Tips paid to Carers are not subject to the platform fee.
5.4 Invoices are issued via Stripe Invoicing on **net-14** payment terms by default. The Customer's default term may be amended in writing.
5.5 **Late payment** accrues interest at **4% above the Bank of England base rate** (UK customers) or **4% above the Wall Street Journal Prime Rate** (US customers), accruing daily.
5.6 The Customer must raise invoice disputes within **14 days** of the invoice date; undisputed amounts must be paid on time.
5.7 SpecialCarer may suspend new Bookings while invoices are overdue.

## 6. SpecialCarer obligations

6.1 SpecialCarer will:
  (a) verify Carers' identity and Right to Work in the UK / right to work in the US;
  (b) require an Enhanced DBS check (UK) or Checkr-equivalent (US) before a Carer can accept Bookings;
  (c) keep ongoing record of Carer compliance and re-check at appropriate intervals;
  (d) target **99.5% Platform availability** measured monthly, excluding scheduled maintenance with 24h notice;
  (e) use commercially reasonable efforts to find a replacement Carer within **2 hours** of a no-show, where Carer availability allows.

## 7. Customer obligations

7.1 The Customer will:
  (a) provide accurate Service User information sufficient for safe care delivery;
  (b) ensure each Carer has a safe working environment compliant with UK / US health-and-safety law;
  (c) comply with applicable safeguarding law (e.g. Care Act 2014, Children Act 1989, US state equivalents);
  (d) supervise Carers as required by the relevant care regime;
  (e) not retaliate against a Carer who raises a safeguarding concern.

7.2 **Anti-poaching.** The Customer will not engage any Carer first found through the Platform off-platform during the term of this Agreement and for **12 months** thereafter, except via SpecialCarer. If breached, the Customer will pay liquidated damages equal to **25% of the Carer's annual booked value** (12 months trailing, or annualised if shorter) per affected Carer. The parties agree this is a genuine pre-estimate of loss.

## 8. Liability

8.1 Subject to clause 8.2, **each party's total aggregate liability** under this Agreement in any 12-month period is capped at **the total Fees paid (or payable) under this Agreement in the 12 months preceding the event giving rise to the claim**.

8.2 The cap in 8.1 does **not** apply to:
  (a) fraud or fraudulent misrepresentation;
  (b) wilful misconduct;
  (c) breach of confidentiality (section 10);
  (d) infringement of the other party's IP (section 11);
  (e) breach of data protection law (where the DPA applies);
  (f) death or personal injury caused by negligence;
  (g) liabilities that cannot lawfully be limited.

8.3 Neither party is liable for indirect or consequential loss, loss of profits, loss of business or loss of goodwill except where excluded from the cap by 8.2.

## 9. Insurance

9.1 SpecialCarer holds and maintains **£5m Public Liability** insurance and **£1m Professional Indemnity** insurance (or US equivalents) for the duration of this Agreement. Certificates of insurance are available on request.
9.2 The Customer holds and maintains insurance appropriate for its care service, including employer's liability where required by law.

## 10. Confidentiality

10.1 Each party will keep the other party's Confidential Information confidential for the term and **5 years thereafter**, and only use it to perform this Agreement.
10.2 "Confidential Information" excludes information already public, independently developed, or required to be disclosed by law (with notice where lawful).

## 11. Intellectual property

11.1 Each party retains ownership of its pre-existing IP.
11.2 SpecialCarer owns the Platform, including any improvements made during the Agreement.
11.3 The Customer owns its data and Service User information.

## 12. Data protection

12.1 The DPA at **Schedule 1** governs all processing of personal data under this Agreement. The DPA prevails over the MSA on data-protection matters.
12.2 The Customer is the **Controller** for Service User data; SpecialCarer is the **Processor**.

## 13. Force majeure

13.1 Neither party is liable for delay or failure caused by an event outside its reasonable control, provided it gives notice and uses reasonable efforts to mitigate.

## 14. Subcontracting and assignment

14.1 SpecialCarer may subcontract the delivery of care to vetted Carers; this is the principal mode of delivery.
14.2 SpecialCarer may assign or novate this Agreement to a successor entity within the All Care 4 U Group Ltd corporate group, including a future SpecialCarer Ltd, on written notice.
14.3 The Customer may not assign without SpecialCarer's prior written consent (not to be unreasonably withheld).

## 15. Anti-bribery and modern slavery

15.1 Each party will comply with the **Bribery Act 2010** (or equivalent local law) and the **Modern Slavery Act 2015**.
15.2 SpecialCarer's Modern Slavery statement is available at specialcarer.com/legal.

## 16. Notices

16.1 Ordinary notices may be sent by email to the addresses on each party's account.
16.2 Notices of breach, termination or claim must be sent **by post AND email** to the registered office of the recipient.

## 17. Governing law and jurisdiction

17.1 For Customers domiciled in the **United Kingdom**: this Agreement is governed by the laws of **England & Wales** and the parties submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of England & Wales.
17.2 For Customers domiciled in the **United States**: this Agreement is governed by the laws of the Customer's home state, with non-exclusive jurisdiction in the state and federal courts located in that state.

## 18. Entire agreement

18.1 This Agreement (including the DPA at Schedule 1, the Fees at Schedule 2 and the Service Levels at Schedule 3) is the entire agreement on its subject. It supersedes any prior agreement.

## Schedule 1 — Data Processing Addendum

The DPA referenced at section 12 is incorporated by reference and is the document titled **"Data Processing Addendum (DPA)"**, version `dpa-v1.0-2026-05`, signed alongside this MSA.

## Schedule 2 — Fees

- Carer rates: as displayed on each Carer's profile in the Platform at the time of Booking.
- Platform fee: 25% of Carer's hourly rate, deducted from Carer's payout.
- No surcharge to the Customer.
- Stripe Invoicing fees: charged to the Customer via Stripe.
- Tips: 0% platform fee.

## Schedule 3 — Service Levels

- Platform availability target: 99.5% measured monthly, excluding scheduled maintenance announced ≥24h in advance.
- Carer no-show response: replacement found within 2 hours where availability allows.
- Trust & Safety acknowledgement: within 4 business hours.
